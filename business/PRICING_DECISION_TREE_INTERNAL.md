# PRICING_DECISION_TREE_INTERNAL

This document is internal and intended for pricing discipline, not publication.

## Decision tree

**Is the buyer an individual consultant?**  
→ Tier 2  
→ Standard path: Gumroad / direct license  
→ Baseline: **$147 / year**

**Is the buyer a small company (≤50 people) using the framework internally or in limited delivery work?**  
→ Tier 2+  
→ Negotiated annual license  
→ Baseline band: **$500 / year**

**Is the buyer a company with >50 people or multi-team use?**  
→ Tier 3  
→ Negotiated annual agreement  
→ Baseline band: **$2,000–$5,000 / year**

**Is the buyer an AI platform, tooling vendor, or workflow platform?**  
→ Tier 3  
→ Requires explicit embedding, agent-grounding, and audit-rights clauses  
→ Baseline band: **$5,000–$15,000 / year**

**Is the buyer an academic institution?**  
→ Default position: AGCL-1.0 community use with strict attribution  
→ If structured operational use is requested: attribution agreement or reduced-fee custom tier

**Is the buyer government / defense / regulated critical infrastructure?**  
→ Tier 3 + custom terms  
→ Expect longer cycle, more formal scope definition, and higher diligence

## When to walk away

Walk away when the buyer:
- wants to resell or white-label without negotiating;
- wants AI embedding rights but refuses auditability or attribution;
- treats the framework as “public domain because it is on GitHub”;
- wants broad internal deployment but pays like an individual seat;
- tries to convert a commercial use case into “research.”

## When to offer community access as a trial

Offer community access, not commercial discounting, when:
- the person is a researcher likely to publish and cite;
- the use is truly non-commercial and exploratory;
- the relationship can increase authority signals without consuming support time.

## How to handle “Can I see it before buying?”

Answer:
**Yes. The community edition is already public.**
The difference is not whether they can read the protocols.
The difference is whether they have the right to use them commercially.

That sentence keeps the value attached to the license, not to artificial secrecy.
