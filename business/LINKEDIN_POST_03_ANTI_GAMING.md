# LINKEDIN_POST_03_ANTI_GAMING

Most evaluation systems claim to be rigorous until someone learns how to feed them the same signal in five different wrappers.

That is why this repository has anti-gaming gates.

One of the most important is the integrity pressure around artifact reuse and self-referential approval.

What does that look like in practice?

A candidate submits:
- one strong artifact,
- two manager notes,
- one architecture memo,
- and then maps the same core work across multiple domain claims.

A normal review often rewards that as breadth.
This framework asks whether the breadth is real or whether one piece of evidence is being stretched beyond admissible support.

The same thing happens with internal promotion loops.
The author writes the artifact.
The manager signs off.
The same sign-off is later used as proof of independent validation.
That is not independent validation. It is a closed approval circuit.

The repository includes a worked example at the SE level that shows the mechanics directly:
input bundle, result object, and trace.

GitHub:
https://github.com/neuron7x/kriterion-security-protocols

If your promotion process cannot detect when the reviewer and the proof chain are effectively the same system, it is not rigorous. It is just confident.
